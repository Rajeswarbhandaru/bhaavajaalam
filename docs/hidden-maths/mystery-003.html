The Hidden Formula

The thermostat does not aim for a single number. It aims for a comfortable zone.

Set Point ± Dead Band = Control Zone
24 ± 1 = 23–25 °C

This tiny buffer prevents the compressor from turning on and off every few seconds. It saves electricity and reduces wear.

Explanation

How the Numbers Work

1

The Set Point

You dial in 24°C on the remote. This is the target temperature — the center of the comfort zone.

24°C
2

The Dead Band

Engineers add a buffer of ±1°C. The AC ignores tiny fluctuations inside this band. It is tolerance, not laziness.

±1°C
3

Turn Off

When room temp drops to 23°C (set point minus dead band), the compressor stops. The fan may still run.

24 − 1 = 23
4

Turn On

When room temp rises to 25°C (set point plus dead band), the compressor restarts. The cycle repeats.

24 + 1 = 25
Applications

Where Else Is This Hiding?

The same control zone logic keeps every temperature-sensitive device from oscillating wildly.

🧊

Refrigerator

The fridge stops at 3°C and restarts at 6°C. The dead band keeps your food safe without burning out the motor.

🍳

Oven

An oven overshoots its target by a few degrees, then waits. The average temperature inside is what matters, not the exact moment.

🚿

Water Heater

Your geyser heats to 65°C, then waits until 55°C before reheating. That 10°C buffer saves massive energy.

🌡️

Incubator

Hospital baby incubators use dead bands as small as 0.1°C. Precision depends on the tolerance the patient can survive.

🧹

Electric Iron

The thermostat in an iron cycles on and off around the set temperature. The dead band stops the relay from chattering.

🌿

Greenhouses

Climate-controlled farms use the same zone logic. A 2°C buffer prevents heaters and coolers from fighting each other.
