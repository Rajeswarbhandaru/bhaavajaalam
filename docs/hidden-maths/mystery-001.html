Discovery

The Hidden Formula

You have been measuring speed all along — without knowing the formula.

Distance ÷ Time = Speed
20 ÷ 0.5 = 40 m/s

This simple relationship is hiding inside every moving object on Earth. You just discovered it yourself.

Explanation

How the Numbers Work

1

The Pitch

A standard cricket pitch is exactly 20.12 metres long. This is the distance the ball must travel.

2

The Time

From release to batsman, the ball takes roughly 0.45 seconds. Time it yourself and you will see.

0.45s
3

Speed in m/s

Divide distance by time: 20.12 ÷ 0.45 = 44.7 m/s

4

Convert to km/h

Multiply by 3.6: 44.7 × 3.6 = 161 km/h. That is why a fast bowler is terrifying.

161 km/h
Applications

Where Else Is This Hiding?

The same formula is everywhere. Once you see it, you cannot unsee it.

🚗

Car Speedometers

Your car measures how many kilometres it covers in one hour. Same formula, different scale.

✈️

Aircraft Landing

Pilots need exact speed to touch down safely. Radar and distance sensors do the same math.

🌊

Tsunami Warnings

Scientists track how fast the wave travels across the ocean to predict arrival time on shore.

🚀

Rocket Launches

Escape velocity is the speed needed to break free from Earth. It is still just distance over time.

🔊

Sound and Light

The speed of sound is 343 m/s. The speed of light is 300,000,000 m/s. Same formula, different speeds.

🏃

Sprint Records

Usain Bolt ran 100m in 9.58s. That makes his average speed 10.4 m/s or 37.6 km/h.
