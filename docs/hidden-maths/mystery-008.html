Discovery

The Hidden Formula

The Luhn algorithm is not encryption. It is a checksum. It proves the number is structured correctly — but does not prove it is yours.

Digits × Weights + Sum ≡ 0 (mod 10)
Σ ( dᵢ × wᵢ ) ≡ 0 mod 10

Once the weighted sum is divisible by 10, the card number is valid. The ATM runs this in a thousandth of a second.

Explanation

How the Numbers Work

1

Double Every Second

Starting from the right, double every second digit. This catches adjacent transposition errors — if two digits swap, the check usually fails.

... 8 7 6 5 → 16 7 12 5
2

Subtract 9

If doubling gives 10 or more, subtract 9. This keeps every digit as a single digit. The math is equivalent to summing the digits of the product.

16 → 7, 12 → 3
3

Sum and Mod 10

Add all digits. Take the remainder when divided by 10. The check digit makes this remainder exactly zero. That is the validation.

Sum mod 10 = 0
4

PIN Hash

The ATM does not store your PIN. It stores a hash — a scrambled number that cannot be reversed. Even the bank cannot read your PIN.

PIN → Hash → Compare
Applications

Where Else Is the Check Digit Hiding?

Checksums are everywhere. They protect long numbers from the small errors humans make every day.

📦

Barcodes

Every retail barcode ends with a check digit. The scanner recalculates it from the product code. If the laser misreads, the beep fails.

📖

ISBN Books

Every book's ISBN ends with a digit that validates the publisher and title code. It catches ordering errors before the book is shelved.

🏥

Medical IDs

Hospital patient numbers and medication codes use check digits. A wrong digit prevents a drug from reaching the wrong patient.

✈️

Airline Tickets

Ticket numbers and boarding pass codes contain checksums. The gate scanner rejects invalid tickets before the plane loads.

📱

IMEI Numbers

Your phone's IMEI has a check digit. Networks verify it before connecting the device. Stolen phones are blocked by invalidating the checksum.

🌐

IP & MAC Addresses

Network packets carry checksums. If a wire flips a bit, the receiver detects the error and requests the packet again.
