Discovery

The Hidden Formula

You have been shifting gears your whole life. The math was hiding in the teeth.

Front Teeth ÷ Rear Teeth = Gear Ratio
42 ÷ 14 = 3 ratio

This ratio decides how far you travel with each push of the pedal. It is multiplication in disguise.

Explanation

How the Numbers Work

1

The Chain

The chain connects the front and rear gears. One full chain link passes for every tooth on either gear.

2

Front Gear

When you pedal once, the front gear turns once. If it has 42 teeth, it pulls 42 links of chain.

42T
3

Rear Gear

The rear gear must take all 42 links. If it has 14 teeth, it turns 3 times to swallow them.

42 ÷ 14 = 3
4

Mechanical Advantage

A big ratio (3:1) gives speed on flat roads. A small ratio (1:1) gives easy power on hills. You choose.

3:1 = Fast
Applications

Where Else Is This Hiding?

Gears are everywhere. Once you know the ratio, you see them in every machine.

🚗

Car Gearbox

First gear is low ratio for power. Fifth gear is high ratio for speed. The clutch just changes the gear pair.

🚛

Truck Differentials

Heavy trucks use tiny gears to multiply engine force. Without ratios, trucks could not move from a standstill.

⌚

Mechanical Watches

A watch hand turns once per hour. The gears between the spring and the hand divide that by 60, then 60 again.

🎡

Ferris Wheels

A small motor spins a tiny gear that drives a massive wheel. The ratio lets a small motor lift thousands of kilograms.

🌬️

Wind Turbines

The blades spin slowly. A gearbox inside multiplies that to thousands of RPM for the generator. Same ratio math.

🚴

All Bicycles

Road bikes use 52:11 for speed. Mountain bikes use 28:42 for climbing. The ratio is the only difference that matters.
