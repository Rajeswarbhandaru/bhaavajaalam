Discovery

The Hidden Formula

You have been firing rockets. The path was never random. Gravity shapes every flight into a parabola.

Velocity² × sin(2×Angle) ÷ Gravity = Range
50² × sin(90°) ÷ 9.8 = 255 m

This formula is why artillery tables, basketball shots, and rocket stages all rely on the same hidden curve.

Explanation

How the Numbers Work

1

Horizontal Speed

The rocket leaves the pad at an angle. The sideways part of its velocity is v × cos(θ). This part never changes — no air resistance.

v × cos(θ)
2

Vertical Speed

The upward part is v × sin(θ). Gravity pulls this downward at 9.8 m/s every second. The climb slows, stops, then reverses.

v × sin(θ)
3

Time in Air

How long until the rocket comes back down? The time depends only on the upward speed. Higher vertical speed means longer flight.

t = 2v×sin(θ) ÷ g
4

Range

Distance = horizontal speed × time. Multiply everything out and you get the elegant formula: v² sin(2θ) ÷ g. That is the parabola.

v² sin(2θ) ÷ g

Where Else Is the Parabola Hiding?

Gravity draws the same curve everywhere. Once you see the parabola, you cannot stop seeing it.
