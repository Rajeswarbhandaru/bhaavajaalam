Welcome to Car Anatomy

Click on any system in the diagram above or use the tabs below to explore how each part of a car works. Every system is connected to others — when one fails, the whole car suffers.

Tip: In the Build Lab, try removing parts and watch the diagnostics to see which systems fail first.

Engine

The engine converts fuel energy into mechanical motion. It is the heart of the car. Without it, the car cannot move.

  • Purpose: Burns fuel to create power that turns the wheels.
  • Connected to: Gearbox, radiator, battery, fuel tank.
  • Common failure: Overheating when the radiator is too small.
  • Symptom: Car stalls, temperature gauge rises, power drops.

Battery

The battery stores electrical energy. It starts the engine and powers lights, sensors, and the ignition system.

  • Purpose: Provide electrical energy for starting and accessories.
  • Connected to: Engine, starter motor, lights, electronics.
  • Common failure: Weak charge or too much drain.
  • Symptom: Car does not start, lights are dim.

Radiator

The radiator removes heat from the engine coolant. Without enough cooling, the engine overheats and stops.

  • Purpose: Dissipate engine heat through coolant and air flow.
  • Connected to: Engine, coolant pump, fans.
  • Common failure: Undersized radiator for the engine heat load.
  • Symptom: Temperature rises, engine stalls, warning lights.

Brakes

The brake system converts motion into heat through friction. It must stop the car safely even with full passenger load.

  • Purpose: Stop the car and hold it stationary.
  • Connected to: Wheels, brake pedal, hydraulic lines.
  • Common failure: Brake force too small for vehicle mass.
  • Symptom: Long stopping distance, brake fade, unsafe.

Wheels

Wheels transfer engine power to the road. They support the vehicle, absorb shocks, and provide steering response.

  • Purpose: Transfer power, support weight, enable movement.
  • Connected to: Brakes, suspension, axle, transmission.
  • Common failure: Worn tires, misalignment, weak bearings.
  • Symptom: Vibration, poor grip, uneven wear.

Steering

The steering system converts wheel rotation into directional change. It must be precise and responsive.

  • Purpose: Control the direction of the front wheels.
  • Connected to: Wheels, suspension, steering column.
  • Common failure: Loose rack, worn joints, poor alignment.
  • Symptom: Loose steering, wandering, uneven tire wear.

Suspension

The suspension connects the wheels to the chassis and absorbs road bumps. It keeps tires in contact with the road.

  • Purpose: Absorb shocks, maintain tire contact, improve comfort.
  • Connected to: Wheels, chassis, steering, brakes.
  • Common failure: Worn springs, weak dampers, poor geometry.
  • Symptom: Bumpy ride, poor handling, uneven tire wear.

Fuel System

The fuel system stores and delivers fuel to the engine. Consistent delivery is critical for reliable combustion.

  • Purpose: Store fuel and deliver it at correct pressure to the engine.
  • Connected to: Engine, fuel pump, injectors.
  • Common failure: Clogged filter, weak pump, leaks.
  • Symptom: Stalling, loss of power, poor mileage.

Gearbox

The gearbox changes the ratio between engine speed and wheel speed. It lets the car start from standstill and cruise efficiently.

  • Purpose: Multiply engine torque and match speed to load.
  • Connected to: Engine, driveshaft, wheels.
  • Common failure: Worn gears, poor lubrication, syncro wear.
  • Symptom: Grinding, hard shifts, power loss, noise.

Headlights

Headlights illuminate the road ahead. They are essential for night driving and visibility to other drivers.

  • Purpose: Illuminate road and signal presence to others.
  • Connected to: Battery, electrical system, body.
  • Common failure: Burned bulbs, weak battery, wiring issues.
  • Symptom: Dim light, flickering, no light at all.

Chassis

The chassis is the structural frame that holds every other system together. It provides rigidity and crash protection.

  • Purpose: Support all systems, absorb crash forces, maintain shape.
  • Connected to: Suspension, engine, body, wheels.
  • Common failure: Rust, cracks, poor design for load.
  • Symptom: Creaking, misalignment, poor handling.
